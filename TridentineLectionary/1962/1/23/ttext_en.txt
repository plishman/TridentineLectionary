St. Raymond was born in 1175 of a noble Spanish family. In middle life he 
entered the Order of St. Dominic. In 1250 he was called to Rome by Pope 
Gregory IX, who made him his confessor and grand penitentiary. He refused the
position of archbishop and also that of general of his Order, and devoted himself
to labors among infidels. He died in 1275.
Commemoration of St. Emerentiana, Virgin, Martyr: St. Emerentiana is said 
to have been stoned by the pagans, When, while still a catechumen, she was 
discovered praying at the tomb of St. Agnes, her foster-sister.
