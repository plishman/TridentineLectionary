This feast, kept in Tours on January 13 from the end of the Fifth Century, was 
inserted in the Roman Calendar by Pius IX. St. Hilary was a native of Poitiers, 
and his family was one of the most illustrious in Gaul. In the year 353 he was 
chosen bishop of Poitiers. About the year 356 he was banished by order of 
Emperor Constantius. He died in his native city, January 13, 363.
